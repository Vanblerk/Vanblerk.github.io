PROTECTING YOURSELF AND YOUR COMPANY

CORPORATE STRUCTURING

Corporate structuring is the restructuring of existing companies or the registration of new companies as a tool to mitigate risks. The goal is to shield assets from claims of creditors without perjury or tax evasion and to legally safeguard assets against unforeseen occurrences such as debt, divorce, liquidation, sequestration, execution by the sheriff and any other unforeseen circumstances.

LIMITED LIABILITY

Although South African companies have a separate legal persona and limited liability, this limited liability is often circumvented by signing personal surety. A good corporate structure must consider personal sureties and protect the corporation as well as directors in their personal capacity should personal cross sureties be required by creditors and financial institutions. CONTROL YOUR DESTINY

The foundation to any good corporate structure is CONTROL. You must be in control of your destiny. Afterall, starting the business was your idea and you have sacrificed a lot to get to where you are. It is unacceptable to register a company on your spouse, children, friend or family member’s name. A good corporate structure will make use of the separation of ownership, but the stakeholders must still retain control. Keeping control of your structure, even in the event of insolvency, is of utmost importance. Don’t let your blood sweat and tears over all the years be for nothing.
