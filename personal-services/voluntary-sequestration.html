START FRESH & DEBT FREE

Voluntary sequestration is an application that is made to the High Court to surrender your estate to the creditors. The estate initially vests in the Master of the High Court who must appoint a trustee to take control of the insolvent estate. The trustee must sell the assets according to the Insolvency Laws of South Africa and distribute the proceeds to creditors once the administration expenses have been settled.

EFFECTS OF SEQUESTRATION

The effect of a Voluntary Sequestration Court Order is that your creditors can no longer legally claim any payments from you, and they must then submit any claims which they may have to the trustee of your insolvent estate. Once the trustee is appointed, he/she takes control of the estate and executes the administrative tasks needed to finalise any claims against the estate.

WHAT TO EXPECT AFTER YOUR VOLUNTARY SEQUESTRATION APPLICATION SUCCEEDS

  • DEBT FREE

    All debts at the time of sequestration form part of the insolvent estate and the creditors musty accordingly claim monies from the estate. This is handled by the trustee

  • LEGAL ACTIONS STOP

    Sections 20(1)(b) stops all legal action by or against the insolvent in terms of the Insolvency Act

  • SALARY PROTECTED

    Your salary is protected after sequestration and you have your full income at your disposal monthly to pay your living expenses

  • PENSION PROTECTED

    Your pension funds, Retirement annuities and provident funds are protected

  • ASSETS VEST IN INSOLVENT ESTATE

    Vehicles, property and other assets may vest in the insolvent estate

  • CANNOT BE DIRECTOR OF A COMPANY

    According to the Companies Act, you may not be a Director of a Company

  • EXCLUDED FROM CERTAIN PROFESSIONS

    You cannot be a Director, Liquidator, Executor, Sheriff. You may be an Attorney, Auditor, Estate Agent or Quantity Surveyor with permission from the relevant regulatory body

  • LISTED ON CREDIT BUREAUS

    The sequestration will be listed on credit bureaus and as such you will not be creditworthy

  • NOT CREDIT WORTHY

    You may obtain credit during the sequestration period, provided your trustee approves such application and the credit provider is aware that you have been sequestrated

  • EFFECTS LAST UNTIL REHABILITATION

    There is an end made to the sequestration by way of a second application to court for a Rehabilitation order
