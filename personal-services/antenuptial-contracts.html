Antenuptial Contracts

TRUE LOVE AGREEMENTS

Antenuptial contracts play a vital role in asset protection and if used correctly are vital for sound estate planning. An Antenuptial Agreement (ANC) is one of the most important contracts that you could enter into during your lifetime. It serves to protect couples against each other’s liabilities during the marriage. A “prenup” arranges the matrimonial property system of spouses. The purpose is to exclude the community of property and profit and loss as well as the include or exclude the accrual system from the marriage.


IF YOU LOVE YOUR SPOUSE, SIGN A CONTRACT.
IF YOU LOVE YOUR ASSETS, SIGN A CONTRACT.


SOUTH AFRICAN LAW RECOGNISES THREE TYPES OF MARRIAGES:

1. Marriage In Community of Property

A marriage entered into without first concluding an antenuptial contract (“prenup”) is automatically a marriage in community of property. When married in community of property, the estates of both parties are combined into one estate with a 50/50 split of all assets and liabilities in the event of death or in the case of divorce. The parties may not act independently from each other and the other’s permission to enter into agreement which affect the joint estate such buying a car on finance, obtaining credit etc.


2. ANC without Accrual

An ANC (excluding accrual) allows both parties to retain any assets held before, as well as acquired during the marriage. The main goal is to allow both parties to enjoy the legal freedom to act independently and protect your spouse’s estate against any financial risk to which your estate may be exposed.


3. ANC with Accrual

The ANC “with” accrual allows both parties to have independent estates and the ability to act independently. Any asset you had before the marriage remains yours, but both partners are entitled to a 50/50 share in the estate growth of the combined estates from the date of the marriage. Certain assets can be excluded from such growth and a “commencement value” of the respective estates must be established at the start of the marriage. Only upon the dissolution of the marriage through death or divorce are the gains during the marriage shared between them.
