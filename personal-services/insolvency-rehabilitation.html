WHAT IS REHABILITATION?

Insolvency rehabilitation after sequestration enables a person who has been sequestrated to make a fresh start, free from pre-sequestration debts and from the restrictions placed on the insolvent by the sequestration. In simple terms, the rehabilitation puts end to the sequestration, and you are once again “credit worthy” in the eyes of the financial institutions as your name will be removed from the credit bureaus. Think of it as being “GREENLISTED”. This process is typically achieved by application to the High Court, but if you have been sequestrated and have not filed an application for rehabilitation within a period of ten years after the sequestration, you are “automatically” rehabilitated.


What does Rehabilitation mean for me?

Rehabilitation enables you to make a fresh start after the sequestration, free from pre-sequestration debts and free of the restrictions placed on you by the sequestration. It is the proverbial “RESET” button to financial freedom and well-being. Allow us to end the financial restrictions placed on you by the credit bureaus, banks and other financial institutions and be the first to welcome you back to economic well-being.


EFFECT OF REHABILITATION

Section 129 of the Insolvency Act confirms that the rehabilitation of an insolvent shall have the effect of:

  1. Putting an end to the sequestration
  2. Discharging all debts of the insolvent, which were due, or the cause of which had arisen before the sequestration, and which did not arise out of any fraud on his part
  3. Relieving the insolvent of every disability resulting from the sequestration

WHEN DO I QUALIFY FOR REHABILITATION?

The requirements to rehabilitate are very clear and concise in the Insolvency Act. There are several sections in the Act which each have their own circumstances, requirements and time frames under which you can rehabilitate.


1. STATUTORY COMPOSITION - SECTION 119(7) READ WITH SECTION 124(1)

A rehabilitation order may be sought at any time after the sequestration if an insolvent has obtained a certificate from the Master confirming that all of the creditors (not only those who have proven claims in your insolvent estate) have accepted an offer of composition in which payment or security for payment has been made of not less than 50 cents in the Rand for every concurrent claim proven against the estate. The rights of secured creditors (bondholders etc) are not affected.

2. CLAIMS PROVEN IN YOUR INSOLVENT ESTATE - SECTION 124(2) (PROVISO SECTION APPLICABLE)

Section 124(2) has a provisional condition which states that if your trustee’s first liquidation and distribution account (L&D account) has been confirmed by the Master and a period of twelve months after this confirmation has elapsed you can apply for rehabilitation at any time if the Master recommends the rehabilitation. However, you must prove very specific circumstances which must be for general benefit of the community, commerce, your company or for example previously disadvantaged persons.

3. PROVEN CLAIMS IN YOUR INSOLVENT ESTATE ARE NOT FULLY PAID - SECTION 124(2)(a)

If creditors have proven claims against your insolvent estate, which were not settled in full, a rehabilitation applications can be sought after twelve months have elapsed since confirmation by the Master of the first liquidation and distribution account submitted by the trustee, PROVIDED THAT a period of four years must have passed since the date of sequestration.

4. PREVIOUS SEQUESTRATION - SECTION 124(2)(b)

If you have been sequestrated before, an application for rehabilitation can only be sought after three years have elapsed since the date of confirmation of the trustee’s first L&D account, PROVIDED THAT a period of four years must have passed since the date of sequestration.

5. FRAUDULENT ACTS OR CRIMINAL OFFENCE COMMITTED - SECTION 124(2)(c)

Where the insolvent has been previously convicted of a fraudulent act or criminal offence in respect of their previous or current insolvency an application for rehabilitation can only be sought after five years have elapsed since the date of confirmation of the trustee’s first L&D account, PROVIDED THAT a period of four years must have passed since the date of sequestration.

6. NO CLAIMS PROVEN - SECTION 124(3)

A rehabilitation application can be made after a period of 6 months have lapsed from date of sequestration if:

  1. at the time of making the application, no claim has been lodged against the insolvent’s estate;
  2. the insolvent has not been convicted of any fraudulent act in relation to their insolvency; and
  3. the insolvent’s estate has not been sequestrated before.

7. ALL PROVEN CLAIMS PAID IN FULL - SECTION 124(5)

The insolvent may apply for rehabilitation at any time after confirmation by the Master of a plan of distribution providing for the payment in full of all proved claims with interest calculated in terms of the Act and all the costs of sequestration.

8. “AUTOMATIC REHABILITATION” BY EFFLUXION OF TIME - SECTION 127(A)

If you have not applied to the High Court for a rehabilitation order within ten years since the date of sequestration, you rehabilitate automatically. The ten-year period runs from the date of provisional sequestration. However, this will not be the case if the court issues an order that the insolvent will not be automatically rehabilitated.
