Change of Matrimonial Property System

Stay the course, no need for divorce

YOU DO NOT HAVE TO DIVORCE YOUR SIGNIFICANT OTHER!

The Matrimonial Property Act No. 88 of 1984 in section 21 provides that a married couple may jointly apply to Court in order to amend the matrimonial property regime applicable to their marriage, regardless of how and when they were married. According to South African law couples are deemed to be married in community of property, if the spouses have not signed and registered an ante-nuptial contract with the registrar. Thus, creating one joint estate belonging to both spouses in equal undivided shares, increasing the financial risk and exposure in the event of debts, litigation or insolvency of either party.

The parties are free to include any provision in the contract, provided it is not against nature, reason, morality, public policy or prohibited by law and does not conflict with the essential consequences of marriage or calculated to lead to separation or divorce or to take over the powers of the Court as such clauses will be null and void.

You can even register a postnuptial contract even if you already have a prenuptial contract in place. Where a postnuptial contract replaces an existing antenuptial contract, the existing contract or a certified copy thereof must be lodged together with the postnuptial contract to be registered.
