THE SIMPLE DEFINITION OF A TRUST

A trust exists when assets (such as property, furniture, cash, cars, shares etc.) are held by one person (the trustee) on behalf of and for the benefit of another person (the beneficiary) and a duty is placed on the trustee to administer the trust assets in accordance with the trust deed.

In essence, a trust is a contract between three parties, namely the Founder, the trustees and the beneficiaries. The “founder” being the person who is desirous to setup the trust, appoints the “trustee/s” to administer the assets to the benefit of the “beneficiaries”.

A family trust currently remains one of the most flexible instruments to support you with asset protection and/or estate planning which can provide you and your family with many advantages, both in the long and short term. When setting up a trust it is of the utmost importance that it is done correctly, ensuring that the trust documents are clear and unambiguous. Our trust law is still in the process of development and is undergoing considerable refinement through Court judgements and is thus best left to experienced attorneys. Making the right decision requires sound advice and sound advice comes from experience. We have been involved in the drafting and registration of trusts for many years. The most common trust that is registered is the “discretionary family trust”.

Trusts are setup for various reasons and can be adjusted to suit your needs. Most trusts are used for:

  1. Continuity through safeguarding and conservation of assets for the next generation; or
  2. The protection of interests of minors, mentally ill persons, the elderly or disabled beneficiaries; or
  3. Charitable purposes or other special causes.
